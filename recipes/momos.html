Momos Recipe

About Momos

omos are a beloved dumpling dish originating from Tibet and Nepal, now popular across many Asian countries and beyond. These bite-sized parcels are typically made from thin dough wrappers filled with a variety of ingredients, such as minced meat (like chicken or pork), vegetables, or tofu, often mixed with spices and seasonings. Momos are commonly steamed, though they can also be fried or pan-fried, resulting in a soft, chewy texture or a crispy exterior, respectively. They are typically served with a dipping sauce, such as soy sauce or chili sauce, and are a favorite street food and snack known for their delightful blend of flavors and versatility in fillings, making them a cherished culinary treat enjoyed by people around the world.

Ingredients Required

  • All-purpose flour (maida)
  • Water
  • Finely chopped vegetables (cabbage, carrots, bell peppers, mushrooms)
  • Minced garlic and ginger
  • Spring onions
  • Seasonings (soy sauce, salt, pepper, and optional chili sauce or hoisin sauce)
  • A steamer for steaming the momos (a bamboo or metal steamer)
  • A rolling pin for rolling out the dumpling wrappers
  • Cornstarch or flour for dusting while rolling out the wrappers

These ingredients can be adjusted according to your taste and dietary preferences. Momos are versatile, and you can customize the fillings to create different flavors and variations.

Step By Step Guide to prepare Recipe

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and a pinch of salt. Gradually add water and knead the dough until it's smooth and pliable. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Add minced garlic and ginger and saut√© for a minute.
  3. Add all the chopped vegetables and mushrooms. Stir-fry for a few minutes until they become tender.
  4. Add soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  5. Take a small portion of the dough and roll it into a thin circle (about 3-4 inches in diameter).
  6. Place a spoonful of the vegetable filling in the center of the dough circle.
  7. Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Seal the edges by pinching and pleating the dough.
  8. Place the prepared momos on a greased steamer tray. Steam them for about 15-20 minutes until the dough becomes translucent and the momos are cooked through.
  9. In a small bowl, mix together soy sauce, vinegar, sesame oil, minced garlic, and chili flakes (if using).
  10. Garnish the sauce with chopped cilantro or green onions.
  11. Serve the steamed momos hot with the dipping sauce. Enjoy your homemade momos!
