Chaap Recipe

About Chaap Recipe

Chaap is a North Indian vegetarian dish known for its delectable flavors and unique texture. It is typically made from soy or wheat protein, which is marinated in a blend of spices, yogurt, and other seasonings to infuse it with rich flavors. This marinated protein is then grilled or fried, resulting in a meaty and satisfying texture that resembles kebabs. Chaap can be served as a snack, appetizer, or main course, often accompanied by various chutneys and garnished with fresh herbs. Loved by both vegetarians and meat-eaters for its savory taste and meat-like texture, chaap has become a popular dish in North Indian cuisine, showcasing the culinary diversity of the region.

Ingredients Required

  • 250 grams soy chaap sticks
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves for garnish

Step By Step Guide to prepare Recipe

  1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, salt, and oil to create a marinade.
  2. Slit the soy chaap sticks lengthwise and coat them thoroughly with the marinade. Let them marinate for at least 1 hour.
  3. Heat a grill pan or skillet and brush it with oil. Grill the marinated chaap sticks until they are golden and cooked through, turning them occasionally.
  4. Garnish with fresh coriander and mint leaves.
  5. Serve hot with your favorite chutney or as part of a curry. Enjoy your homemade chaap!
